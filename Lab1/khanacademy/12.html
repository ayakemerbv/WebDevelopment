Travel to Almaty

Boasting a rich history, stunning natural beauty, and a thriving arts scene, Almaty offers visitors a unique blend of urban sophistication and picturesque landscapes. Explore its diverse cuisine, bustling markets, and outdoor adventures to experience the essence of this captivating city.

Almaty , the largest city in Kazakhstan, lies nestled at the foothills of the Trans-Ili Alatau mountains. Formerly the capital until 1997, it remains a significant cultural, economic, and financial center in the country. With roots tracing back to ancient times as a Silk Road trading point, the city carries a rich historical tapestry.

Trip advices

If you're planning a trip to Almaty, Kazakhstan, here are some travel tips to enhance your experience:

  1. Visa Requirements:
    Check the visa requirements for your nationality before traveling to Kazakhstan. Ensure that your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your planned departure date.
  2. Currency:
    The local currency is the Kazakhstani Tenge (KZT). It's advisable to have some local currency for small purchases, but credit cards are widely accepted in major establishments.
  3. Language:
    The official language is Kazakh, but Russian is also commonly spoken. English may be understood in tourist areas, but it's helpful to learn a few basic phrases in Russian or Kazakh.
  4. Weather:
    Almaty experiences a continental climate with hot summers and cold winters. Check the weather forecast for your travel dates and pack accordingly.
  5. Transportation:
    Almaty has a well-developed public transportation system, including buses and a metro. Taxis are also available, and ridesharing services may be an option. Ensure that you use official and reputable transportation services.
  6. Safety:
    Almaty is generally considered safe for tourists. However, exercise common travel precautions, such as safeguarding your belongings and avoiding poorly lit or unfamiliar areas at night.
  7. Local Cuisine:
    Try traditional Kazakh dishes, which often include meat, dairy products, and bread. Beshbarmak, manti, and plov are popular local dishes. Also, explore local markets for a variety of fresh produce and traditional snacks.
  8. Cultural Etiquette:
    Respect local customs and traditions. If you are invited to someone's home, it's customary to bring a small gift. Handshakes are a common form of greeting.
  9. Health Precautions:
    Ensure your vaccinations are up-to-date before traveling. It's also wise to have travel insurance that covers medical emergencies.
  10. Explore Nature:
    Take advantage of Almaty's natural beauty. Visit the Medeu Skating Rink, Shymbulak Ski Resort, and Kok Tobe for stunning views of the city and surrounding mountains.
  11. Plan Day Trips:
    Consider day trips to nearby attractions, such as Charyn Canyon, Big Almaty Lake, or Tamgaly Tas, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with ancient rock carvings.

Remember to check for any travel advisories or updates closer to your travel dates. Enjoy your trip to Almaty!
